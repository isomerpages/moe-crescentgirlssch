<p>On 16 February 2026, Crescent Girls’ School came alive with a vibrant
Chinese New Year (CNY) celebration. Instead of the usual pale-yellow uniforms,
a sea of bright red greeted us, filling the campus with festive cheer.
The excitement was especially palpable for the Secondary 1 students, as
it was one of our first major celebrations in the school.</p>
<p>We began the day in our classrooms, watching an engaging and humorous
video about the origins of CNY. The amusingly illustrated characters had
us laughing throughout. This was followed by lively rounds of Kahoot and
Blooket, which wrapped up our form teachers’ interaction time on a high
note.</p>
<p>During recess, parent volunteers treated us to an array of delicious snacks,
including shrimp rolls, pineapple tarts, and fortune cookies. There were
also crowd favourites like nacho chips with cheese and popcorn. A special
highlight was the appearance of the God of Fortune (Cai Shen Ye), portrayed
by a parent volunteer, who delighted everyone by handing out chocolate
coins.</p>
<p>The celebration culminated in the CNY Concert at the school hall. Performances
by the Angklung and Kulintang CCA, Choir CCA, Dance CCA, teachers, and
international students captivated the audience. The lion dance thrilled
the crowd with its powerful drumming and skilful movements, while a vibrant
dragon dance added to the excitement. The atmosphere was electric, and
the celebration was truly a memorable success for all.</p>
